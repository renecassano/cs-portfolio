What is a motherboard?

The motherboard of a computer is generally referred to as the core and heart of a computer. It contains all of the basic components and circuitry of the computer. One of the most common models of motherboards is the AT motherboard, though a newer model; the ATX motherboard exists, and both models have the same basic components.
Motherboards have a decently long history and chain of evolution from throughout the years.
In 1975, Apple introduced their first product, which would become known as the Apple I. The device consisted of keyboard, motherboard, and a display.
Then, in 1987 The Elitegroup Computer Systems Co. became the largest motherboard supplier around. Not long after, in 1989, AsusTek began making graphic cards.

RAM functions in a very similar way, it is where all the current data and apps currently in use are stored. Just like all other parts that make up computers, RAM has gone through a decent amount of evolution. Initially, RAM was asynchronous, but this eventually became too inefficient and couldn’t keep up with how processors were becoming more and more powerful. Thus, the introduction of SDRAM (synchronous dynamic random access memory) in the 1990s allowed for clock speeds to be synchronized. A decade later, another big step in evolution of RAM had to be taken, and DDR RAM (double data rate random access memory) was developed.
