What is a Motherboard?

The motherboard of a computer is generally referred to as the core and heart of a computer. It contains all of the basic components and circuitry of the computer. One of the most common models of motherboards is the AT motherboard, though a newer model; the ATX motherboard exists, and both models have the same basic components.
Motherboards have a decently long history and chain of evolution from throughout the years.
In 1975, Apple introduced their first product, which would become known as the Apple I. The device consisted of a keyboard, motherboard, and a display.
Then, in 1987 The Elitegroup Computer Systems Co. became the largest motherboard supplier around. Not long after, in 1989, AsusTek began making graphic cards.

What is RAM?

One of the most simple ways of explaining is comparing it to short term memory. In people’s brains, short term memory is where the information at hand is stored for a short amount of time, it is how people remember points of a conversation and continue it. RAM functions in a very similar way, it is where all the current data and apps currently in use are stored. Once the computer is turned off, all the data on the RAM is lost and then restored when the computer is turned back on
Just like all other parts that make up computers, RAM has gone through a decent amount of evolution. Initially, RAM was asynchronous, but this eventually became too inefficient and couldn’t keep up with how processors were becoming more and more powerful. Thus, the introduction of SDRAM (synchronous dynamic random access memory) in the 1990s allowed for clock speeds to be synchronized. A decade later, another big step in evolution of RAM had to be taken, and DDR RAM (double data rate random access memory) was developed. Since then, there have been a few incarnations of DDR RAM (DDR2, DDR3, DDR4), each one improving in efficiency from the last

What is a CPU?

The CPU is the part of the computer that is interprets and executes a large number of commands from a computer's other hardware and software. CPU stands for Central Processing Unit, a now very evidently apt name, especially because the CPU has nicknames like "the brains of the computer" . Just about all sorts of technology, including smartphones and flat screen televisions. The central processing unit consists of two main parts: the arithmetic/logic unit and the control unit. The contrl unit has circuitry that sends electrical signals to direct other parts of the computer to execute or carry out some instructions. The arithmetic/logic unit (ALU) executes all arithmetic and logical operations. The ALU can perform addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Logical operations are typically making a comparison and determines whether something is equal to, less than and greater than something else.

What is Hard Drive?

The hard disk drive is the primary hardware data storage piece in a computer. The hard drive is basically made up of circular disks stacked on top of eachother. A small arm type of things moves over the disks and writes information on them. The hard drive can also be referred to as: HDD, fixed drive, fixed disk drive, fixed disk, C drive, etc.

What is BUS?

In the world of computing, a BUS is a series of physical cables, circuits etc. between the different parts of a computer that create a sort of "data highway". There are many types of buses. The different types include frontside Bus, which decides data transfer rate speed and is the main transferway for the CPU, RAM, and various other motherboard parts. Another variant is the system bus that helps with communication between the CPU and memory. Other types of buses are internal, external, expansion, and backside.
